Title: Dorm Room necessities

=== comment ===
You are going to find that you need WAY less than you think you need. One thing that might not be immediately obvious is a mirror, especially if you have an on floor bathroom.

=== comment ===
Extension cords. Window fan if in the freshman dorms. Small trash can. Bedding, command hooks. Laundry bag & detergent. I packed plastic shoes boxes: one for cold/allergy meds/first aid stuff the other one with pencils/stapler/tape/post it's etc. Thin coat & rain coat. Snacks and a few small plastic storage containers for them to kerp bugs at bay. Dust pan/brush. Takes back the heavier winter gear after Thanksgiving break. Packed using the blue IKEA zippered duffle bags so kids knew how many bags they could take depending on what fit in the car. The open top blue bags can be used for the bulkier stuff. Bags double as laundry bags or can be used if you leave stuff over summer in a storage unit. Our son is a rising senior. When he bags it all back between terms, it gets sorted, washed and new or expired items get replaced. All gets repacked and stored in our basement until he he leaves again. He only adds his clothes. Makes going back each term easier. We only live three hours away so this might be different if you have to fly.

=== comment ===
That link includes things that are not on the official packing list, although I don't know what list you have. Wifi was fine, but definitely also bring a long ethernet cord. Some regular printing is included free https://itssc.rpi.edu/hc/en-us/articles/360004259072-Printing-Charges Learn to not leave things to the last minute, as printers might not print correctly when you most need them (especially if you need to print special size design projects). Plenty of people do fine without bringing a printer. Plenty also don't take notes with pen and paper - if professors post notes in advance you can add your notes electronically freehand during the lecture, no need to print them. Also don't buy any textbooks unless the professor says in the first class that you really have to buy them (many are just codes to view pdf of the textbook and be able to submit homework electronically (it could be $150 for that personal code, per class), and for classes that don't need that code some pdfs can be found online for free.

=== comment ===
As per the usual, bring clothes. RPI weather typically warrants a pretty warm start to the year in August and then as winter starts it gets very very cold. It’s a bit irregular. Also an umbrella or raincoat. My freshman year it rained a ton and it also snowed some in February March I think. When it is warm in August and towards the end of the school year though I used to walk around with mini fans in my hand so those could be helpful. No AC so maybe a desk fan. Bring snow boots for the winter, then some sneakers, sandals, maybe some business shoes. (Ccpd closet can lend those out if you need but that is a different topic)

=== comment ===
Other than clothes: Bring general cleaning supplies aka wipes. I usually wipe down everything when I move in. Maybe a febreeze. Bring tissues and such. Freshman year if you have a suite bathroom they usually clean it for you but it doesn’t hurt to bring a swiffer or a small vacuum unless you don’t have space. If you have a floor bathroom bring a shower caddy maybe. I heard the floor bathrooms are typically quiteee gross (idk I avoided them) Keep a stash of trash bags or just small plastic bags (there is usually just a trash or recycling room somewhere in the hall that you can toss things in)

=== comment ===
Bring a laundry bag or basket to leave above the dryer or washer. One because you definitely need it and two because some people are quite impatient and will move all the clothes on top of the machine if clothes aren’t picked up on time, so having a hamper on top would help to make sure things aren’t lost.

=== comment ===
Bring writing utensils and the usual notebooks and binders. Maybe some loose leaf if you take a lot of notes (I found myself running out of paper towards the end of the year pre the online years but it depends and you can always just restock later). A hole puncher or stapler can be useful but if there is no space you can drop by the library for them.

=== comment ===
If you are on the first floor or a room with a tree next to the window. I suggest getting something to keep the window closed because my freshman dorm if a single window was open we would get overrun with spiders every now and then.

=== comment ===
Bring some bedding. I believe they are twin size. Maybe extra long but I don’t remember. You probably just need light blankets initially and then maybe later some more layers. If the bed can be lifted up you can keep things underneath. But it is good for things to be compact because freshman dorms don’t usually have a lot of space to leave luggage sitting off to the side. Maybe bring some hanger

=== comment ===
Bring your typical daily necessities: toothbrush, toothpaste, soap, shampoo, conditioner, towels, loofah maybe Maybe some sandals if floor bathroom.
