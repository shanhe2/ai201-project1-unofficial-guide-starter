=== comment ===
Someone said Ethernet cable, if you have multiple devices, get a small network switch so you can have multiple devices run off of the one wall port. I also recommend getting a dongle so you can use Ethernet on your laptop if the wifi ever is bad (as it sometimes is in the freshman dorms).
Bring some organizational/storage things. Having extra storage means when you get misc items you can put it somewhere and have a better looking and functional room.
I also recommend having a lot of stuff so you can make inventive solutions. Get some duct tape, some rope, zip ties, and maybe something rigid. Now when you need some random thing to maybe hang something or secure something like a table to the side of your bed, you can do that. I made a fan table for my lofted bed out of a shoebox and some zip ties and it was one of the most important things I made
I would also get a cheap multi bit screwdriver set on Amazon.

=== comment ===
A fan even if you are in a room with a/c. A rug if you are in a room with no carpet. Some dishes and a small dish soap. Plastic boxes to keep food and dishes away from mice that are in some dorms. A plastic shoe tray (for the wet boots). Umbrella.
Buy a jacket that is 2 layers (some call it 3 in 1) - a polyester warmer layer and a waterproof outer layer with a hood. You can wear the inner layer as a sweatshirt, the outer layer as a rain jacket, or both layers when it's cold. Get gloves, hat, waterproof boots with good traction. The winter temperatures go up and down a lot, and different building could be heated too much, so wearing layers is key. The campus is open and on a hill, so it gets really windy. Mixed precipitation happens more often than just snow, so walking on ice and slush is the norm.

=== comment ===
Any winter coat that's comfy and wind resistant. Some parts of campus are very windy. I wore hiking boots (merrells) 99% of the time. Unless it snows several feet (which it does once in a while), hiking boots with a couple coats of waterproofing spray is more than good enough. I'd also recommend a scarf, hat, and warm gloves, and maybe a neck warmer that you can pull over the bottom half of your face. The wind + ice shards is seriously brutal at times.
As for room stuff, a small LED desklamp, a washable shower caddy (and do actually wash it or it will get moldy and gross), a microwave/small refrigerator (if you can afford it, both are very nice to have), a comfy chair (I just had a folding papasan chair) and comfy blanket, and lots of command hooks/clips.

=== comment ===
Extension cord if you're in the freshmen dorms as outlets are limited. Small trssh can. Check the rpi website. Somewhere there is a list of " not allowed items ". First aid kit, cold/allergy meds, aspirin. Laundry bag/detergent. If driving to RPI order blue zippered duffle bags from IKEA or Amazon. They last forever, fold up to store ( vs a box), dbl as a laundry bag. The open top totes version is good for bulky items. My kids knew how many bags they could use so everything would fit in the car. If you need to use a storage facility over the summer rather than ship stuff home the bags are this rip stop type of fabric so mice etc can't get in like they would a box. And my son/roommate each had a window fan. Freshmen dorms don't have AC. They put it in the lower windows to keep cool. When the heat went on and it's got stifling they moved the fans to the upper windows and sucked the hot air out. Good luck!

=== comment ===
I went on amazon and got water-proof hiking boots (for snow <4") and then I already had snow boots for SNOW. I also got a parka for when it gets to like 0 degrees which is all-around nice to have. Get one that covers your butt because if you sit down somewhere you don't want to get wet or cold. For when it's warmer, rain jacket and umbrella. Don't bother packing face masks as they give out way too many here for free. Having a fridge and microwave in your room is nice (plus snacks to store/ make in them). WATER FILTER because the water here is beyond sketchy. At BARH it is physically YELLOW AFTER going through the filter a few times for a few weeks. Other dorms aren't much better. If you're in a communal bathroom, cheap flip-flops to shower in to avoid foot fungus and who knows what else. A shower caddy to carry bathroom supplies in and out of the bathroom. A giant sack for laundry is helpful for when you have a lot of clothes and then when you wash bedding. A broom or vacuum to clean. A mirror of some kind because the bathrooms are taken enough to be inconvenient every time you need a mirror (depending on the bathroom style).
