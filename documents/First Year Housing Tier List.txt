Title: First Year Housing

=== comment ===
Really depends on what you value. Most people prefer to not have communal bathrooms so typically ones like Davison and Nugent are most desirable. AC is also something people care about but it only really matters for about 6-7 total weeks. If you look at the SLL website, you can see the comparison between the different dorm types. Most of the traditional dorms won’t have AC and will have communal bathrooms, and most of the suites will have in-room bathrooms and some have AC. Barton is also a solid pick but it is a triple so that discourages some people. BARH was also good from personal experience but it is further from campus since it’s not on freshman hill. Overall, the general consensus picks are typically sharp, nugent and davison. As for the worst ones, I think people have said Cary or Crockett are the worst but most of the freshman 5 are generally all the same.

=== comment ===
You can't live outside RPI residence halls till 3rd year. Exemptions are available for Greek housing for sophomore students. Some organizations offer excellent housing.

=== comment ===
I always assumed most student-athletes get put in BARH, as it's closer to ECAV and the Field House, but things might be different now. Other than that, the selection system is so opaque that I can't tell you anything other than stockpile your luck.
Assuming they're not giving you guys Quad, E-Complex or North (which they did when we were freshmen since there were so many of us), the general tier list is this
Davison and Nugent. Every prospective freshman two years ago absolutely drooled over Davison, and not without reason. AC and a lot of space for a double (250 sq ft). Nugent is just a mirror image of Davison.
Barton. If you're okay with a forced triple that gives you less space (215 sq ft) than a Davison/Warren double, it's literally perfect. If you do end up in Barton, bring back couch elevator, that was a core memory from when I was living in Barton (RIP September 2022-November 2022).
Sharp. Think of it as the anti-Barton. 101 sq ft single suites (so you live in a group but you get your own place to sleep), but a lot of stuff is either missing entirely or stuck in the basement. I've heard a ton of nice things about it, though.
Warren. Doubles with in-room toilets, a good amount of space (231 sq ft) and nearly every amenity. It's nice, but iwith
BARH. You get a dining hall to boot and good space (240 sq ft), but there's a chance you get stuck in a triple no bigger than the doubles. It's also missing a good amount of amenities, and it's further from the center of campus. When you're not allowed to have a car on campus, location is huge.
The Freshman 5 (Bray, Cary, Crockett, Hall, Nason). They're nowhere near as bad as people say, but "least good" is the best way to describe them. The buildings are also the oldest on Freshman Hill. It's pick your poison between a small double (175 sq ft) or a forced triple (260 sq ft), and then pray to god you don't die in the communal toilets. Nason gets a classroom though, so that's a plus.

Sharp is my personal recommendation for freshman housing. Good ac and you get your own private room. It's 1 common room and 4 single rooms per apt in sharp.

All dorms but barh on on what we call freshman hill right next to the dining hall and are about a 5 min walk to campus. Barh adds maybe an extra 5 mins but it’s closer to ECAV, so that could be better?
